# KAIROS Weekly 2026-W17

Frozen as of 2026-04-25.

## Thesis

This week the AI trade spread outward from model supply toward control points. The strongest new signals did not say "buy more compute." They said the next battleground is who owns agent-readable distribution, who can sell AI as finished work instead of software seats, and where defense/autonomy capital is starting to compound before public-market consensus fully catches up.

## Top Kairos Moments

1. Agent-led Growth
   - Fact: Sequoia's Training Data episode with Profound argued that the important shift is not a new front door to the internet, but that agents are now the entity walking through it.
   - View: Distribution advantage moves from ranking pages for humans to becoming legible, citable, and preferred by AI agents.
2. AI-native Services
   - Fact: Sequoia's "Services: The New Software" argued that the next large AI winners may sell completed work rather than tools.
   - View: Outcome-priced AI services can attack both seat-based SaaS and labor-heavy service firms, with margins improving as models improve.
3. Defense Autonomy
   - Fact: a16z announced an investment in Ulysses on 2026-04-16, Portal Space Systems announced a $50M Series A on 2026-04-09 with ARK Invest participating, and ARK's site now states the ARK Venture Fund invested in Portal's Series A.
   - View: Defense autonomy is becoming a software-plus-hardware capital stack, not a niche hardware story.

## Capital Movement

- ARKK sold AMD for about $32.7M and bought AMZN for about $32.3M on 2026-04-24, a notable rotation from pure compute exposure toward a broader distribution and platform surface.
- ARKW bought NFLX on 2026-04-17 while selling BLSH, CRCL, and CRWV, which looks less like indiscriminate risk-on and more like selective re-underwriting.
- Portal's $50M Series A is the cleanest cross-over capital signal this week: venture, defense, and space mobility money are converging around maneuverability and autonomy.

## Point To Map

The map is shifting from "AI capex" to "AI changes who captures demand." Compute and power still matter, but the incremental edge is moving into distribution, workflow ownership, and mission-critical autonomy.
